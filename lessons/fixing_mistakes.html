Dictation and fixing mistakes

Whether you are typing or using voice to make stuff appear on your screen, you are bound to make some errors along the way. You are going to pronounce a tongue twister, and this will probably make some mistakes. This practice session is going to be all about fixing those mistakes. Dictation is simple enough. Starting a sentence with the word say or phrase will turn on dictation mode and type out all the words you have said afterwards. If you just want to add a single word, you can start the word using the phrase word as well. This doesn't always turn out perfectly as you want, so you need ways to fix these mistakes.

The first way you can fix mistakes is the phrase undo that, this will undo the last action you did. redo that will undo the undoing. For smaller mistakes, you might want to use the phrase delete which will press backspace to remove the last letter or your selection.

Now deleting single letters is fine and all, but you might get tired saying delete over and over again if you want to delete a long word or sentence. For this, you have a range of clear commands.
clear line for example will clear your entire current line. But you can also do a ton of things with your current cursor using direction words at the end. You can put a direction like left, up, right and down after a clear command. Saying clear word left will clear the previous word, and clear word right will clear the next one. But you can also say things like clear way up to delete everything between your current cursor and the start of the file.
Try playing around with commands clear (direction) and clear way (direction) to see what they do.
